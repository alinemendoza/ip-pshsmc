Overview of IP

What is Intellectual Property or IP?

Intellectual property as defined by the Intellectual Property Office of the Philippines or IPOPhil, refers to a distinct creations or product of the mind. This can be an invention (patent / utility model), a design (industrial design), a brand name (trademark), or a literary and artistic work (copyright).


image by: by ALMendoza


Why is it Important to Protect your Intellectual Property? So...
  • You can prevent others from using your work without permission.
  • You can make money out of it by selling it or earn royalties by lincensing it.
  • You can defend yourself in court in case others use it without your permission.
  • You can have opportunity to commercialize it.

Can I Patent My Work?

Ask yourself the following five (5) questions before you apply for a patent:
  1. Is it patentable?
  2. Is it new or can it be called a novelty?
  3. Is it inventive?
  4. Is it useful?
  5. Has it not been used before or not have prior use?

Three (3) Conditions from IPOPhil for an invention to be patentable
  1. It has to be new
  2. It involves inventive steps
  3. It has industrial application.

Patent Limitations

Term of Protections

According to IPOPhil, the term of a patent shall be twenty (20) years from the filing date of the application and maintained yearly, starting from the 5th year.


Other Limitations
  • Geographical Limits - Protection is by Country
  • Fees - The Patent has to be maintained
  • Defined Scope or It is protected only based on submitted claims
  • First to File System not First to Invent
