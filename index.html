Overview of IP

What is Intellectual Property or IP?


Intellectual property as defined by the Intellectual Property Office of the Philippines or IPOPhil, refers to a distinct creations or product of the mind. This can be an invention (patent / utility model), a design (industrial design), a brand name (trademark), or a literary and artistic work (copyright).


image by: by ALMendoza


Why is it Important to Protect your Intellectual Property? So...


  • You can prevent others from using your work without permission.
  • You can make money out of it by selling it or earn royalties by lincensing it.
  • You can defend yourself in court in case others use it without your permission.
  • You can have opportunity to commercialize it.

How Can I Protect My Work?

Ask yourself the following five (5) questions before starting the process:


  1. Is it new or can it be called a novelty?
  2. Is it inventive?
  3. Is it useful?
  4. Has it not been used before or not have prior use?

Three (3) Conditions from IPOPhil for an invention to be patentable


  1. It has to be new
  2. It involves inventive steps
  3. It has industrial application.

Protection Limitations

Term of Protections


According to IPOPhil, the term of a patent shall be twenty (20) years from the filing date of the application and maintained yearly, starting from the 5th year.


Other Limitations


  • Geographical Limits - Protection is by Country
  • Fees - The Patent has to be maintained
  • Defined Scope or It is protected only based on submitted claims
  • First to File System not First to Invent

PSHSS IP Policy

The Philippine Science High School System Intellectual Property Policy or PSHSS IP Policy were approved last October 30, 2020 and it aims to achieve the following objectives:

  1. To ensure that the lntellectual Properties (IPs) of the PSHS System such as trademarks, utility models, industrial designs, copyrights and patents are accorded proper protection of law;
  2. To establish clear guidelines for determjning the rights and obligations of the PSHS System and the creators of intellectual properties and their sponsors, with respect to works created, or invented, or discovered at PSHS;
  3. To promote and support the PSHS system's role and performance as a Government Funding Agency (GFA) and a Research and Development Institute (RDI) pursuant to chapter ll (Sections 1-2) of DOST Administrative Order No. 016 series of 2019 or "DOST Intellectual Property Policy" and DOST Administrative Order No. 018 series of 2019 or "The lnteltectual Property Management protocol of the Department of Science and Technology-Government Funding Agencies, as Amended,"
  4. To establlsh guidelines for resolving conflicting interests among members of the PSHS Research community, students, and personnel with respect to Intellectual Property Rights (IPRs).
